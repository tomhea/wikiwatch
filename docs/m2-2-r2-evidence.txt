=== Interpretation ===

The H1 title at the very top of the article (lines 0-4) gets wrapped to w=64 px wide
sub-lines because its natural screen position is near the top bezel where the
horizontal chord is only ~114 px wide; subtracting 25 px padding on each side
leaves 64 px of safe text width.

Lines in the middle of the article (line[9] onward in the example) reach the
central horizontal band when scrolled there, so they wrap to w=366 px - the
full chord minus 50 px total padding (25 each side).

Lines near the end of the article (line[55+]) get narrowed again because their
natural screen position (at scrollY=maxScroll) sits near the bottom bezel.

=== Differences from M2.1 ===

M2.1 used a single full-width budget for ALL lines (dc.getWidth() - 4), assuming
the user would scroll any line into the central band to read it clearly - which
required an extended scroll range past content boundaries.

M2.2 reverts to the standard scroll range [0, contentH - screenH] and instead
WRAPS each line at the chord-width available at its natural screen position.
The first and last lines of the article display CLEANLY at their natural
position (no scroll past content needed). Middle lines still use full diameter.

This matches the user's spec: "actual start/end of each article should be in a
'safe width' so that it will be displayed correctly on the top/bottom of the
watch circular screen".
