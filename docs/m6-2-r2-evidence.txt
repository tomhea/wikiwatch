Manual verification protocol (touchscreen, on the sim)
=======================================================

For each scenario below, expected stdout is the M5 rank line(s) that appear
after each keypress, plus what the keyboard center should render.

Scenario A — ASCII punctuation appears in the search results
-------------------------------------------------------------
With empty buffer, tap ש. Expected:
    M5 rank: buf='ש' top=[שלום,שבת] more=34 total=36

Notice total=36 (was 30 in M5.2..M6.1). All 6 new entries match the ש
prefix. Tap "▼ 34 more" → ResultsView opens with all 36 ש-prefix entries.
Scroll down → the 5 new punctuation-bearing titles render with their ASCII
" / ' / - INTACT (e.g. ש"ס on its own row, שב"ק, ש"ץ, ש"י-עגנון,
שלום-בית, ש'אהרון). The display preserves the originals.

Scenario B — Gershayim (") ignored during matching
---------------------------------------------------
Buffer="שס" (without "). The title ש"ס in the corpus normalizes to "שס"
during ranking. Expected:
    M5 rank: buf='שס' top=[ש"ס,...] more=0 total=1   (or more if substrings hit too)

Tap "ש" then DIGITS → expansion shows 13 cells (0..9 + " + ' + -). Tap ".
Buffer becomes "ש"". Now tap ס:
    M5 rank: buf='ש"ס' top=[ש"ס] more=0 total=1

Both "שס" and "ש\"ס" queries match the same title.

Scenario C — Makaf (-) treated as space
-----------------------------------------
Type "שלום בית" (with space). Expected:
    M5 rank: buf='שלום בית' top=[שלום-בית] more=0 total=1

The hyphen in שלום-בית normalizes to space during matching, so the
space-separated query hits.

Likewise, "שלום-בית" (with -) should match the same article. Both queries
collapse to the same normalized form "שלום בית".

Scenario D — Body-content fallback (tier 3)
---------------------------------------------
Type "ראשי תיבות" (the phrase appearing in several of the new article
bodies but in NO article title). Expected:
    M5 rank: buf='ראשי תיבות' top=[ש"ס,שב"ק] more=N total=N+2

The matches are body-only (the phrase appears in shas, shabak, shatz,
shai-agnon, sh-aharon bodies). Tier-3 results appear after any title hits
and are popularity-sorted within that tier. Tapping a result opens the
reader on that article (per the existing M5 push-on-tap flow).

Scenario E — Title hit ranks BEFORE body hit (cross-tier)
----------------------------------------------------------
Type "שמואל". Article "shmuel" has title שמואל (title-prefix tier 1). The
sh-aharon body mentions "שמואל אהרון" (body tier 3). Expected:
    M5 rank: buf='שמואל' top=[שמואל,sh-aharon-title-here] more=0 total=2

The title-match wins ordering regardless of relative popularity.
