M2.4 evidence — narrow-tail only at the absolute last sub-line + right-justify
middle lines + onUpdate skip-ahead optimization.

=== Per-line widths (one-off diagnostic from a build with println) ===

M2_4 N=53 contentH=2321
  [0]   w=160       <- first line (H1 sub 1, center-justified)
  [1]   w=250       <- second line (H1 sub 2, center-justified)
  ... (middle lines, all w=416, right-justified at screenW - 25) ...
  [N-4] w=416       <- middle width near the bottom (right-justified)
  [N-3] w=416       <- middle width
  [N-2] w=250       <- penultimate (center-justified)
  [N-1] w=160       <- last (center-justified)

Compare M2.3's last-paragraph widths: every sub-line beyond index 1 in the
last raw got w=160 because LineWrap.wrapToWidths.defaultWidth fell back to
the LAST entry of widths=[250, 160]. With the longer last paragraph in
M2.4, M2.3 would have produced 5-6 narrow sub-lines at the bottom.

M2.4 introduces LineWrap.wrapWithNarrowTail(text, charW, middleW, secondW,
edgeW). Algorithm:
1. Reverse-pack the LAST sub-line at edgeWidth (greedy from text end).
2. Reverse-pack the PENULTIMATE at secondWidth.
3. Forward-pack the REMAINDER at middleWidth.
Only the last 2 sub-lines are narrow; everything before stays at middle.

=== Justify rules ===

In wikiwatchView.onUpdate:
  if (line.w >= middleWidth)  draw right-justified at x = screenW - 25
  else                        draw center-justified at x = screenW / 2

Narrow first/last sub-lines sit in the narrow horizontal chord at top/bottom
of the round screen - center-justify keeps the text inside that chord.
Middle sub-lines have the full diameter available - right-justify anchors
the right edge of the text 25 px from the screen edge (clean finger margin
for users who scroll with their right hand), and the wrap budget of
screenW lets text bleed up to 25 px past the left edge as the user explicitly
requested ("use that. Allow yourself to use 'a bit too much' if it makes
the line nicer").

=== onUpdate optimization ===

The original onUpdate iterated all _lines unconditionally. M2.4 splits the
loop in two:
1. Skip-ahead while-loop: advance i past lines fully above the viewport.
2. Draw while-loop: render until the first line at or below the viewport
   bottom, then break.

For a 50+ line article and a viewport showing ~6 lines, this drops the
per-frame work from ~50 iterations to ~6. Helps scroll smoothness during
fast drags.
