M5.2 R2 evidence — multi-line buffer + separators + 30 ש-fixtures + lazy article layout

M5.2 bundles 4 distinct UX changes, each visible on the watch:
  1. Separator lines (1 px, 67 px wide, DK_GRAY) between consecutive
     suggestion rows + before the "▼ N more" footer.
  2. Multi-line (2-row) buffer band restored (bandH 30 -> 64) per user's
     request — suggestion count drops 3 -> 2 to make room.
  3. 30 fixture articles all starting with ש (was 3). Version-aware
     installer auto-migrates from any prior version on next launch.
  4. Lazy article layout: first paint is instant; remaining content lays
     out via background timer ticks; loading "..." marker visible until
     done.

Tap "▼ 28 more":
  - Full-screen ResultsView (M5.1) pushed, listing all 30 titles in

        שיר לשלום מאת חיים נחמן ביאליק ונועה קירל
    (deliberately anachronistic per the user's wrap-stress-test request).
  - Tap any row → article reader pushes.

Tap "שלום" suggestion (in keyboard) OR "שלום" row (in ResultsView):
  - Article reader (wikiwatchView) appears INSTANTLY — first ~12 raw
    lines are laid out in the first onUpdate (typical 2 screens of body
    content). The "..." marker appears at the bottom of the screen,
    centered.
  - Background timer (Timer.Timer, _layoutTimer field) fires every
    80 ms. Each fire calls WatchUi.requestUpdate, triggering another
    onUpdate where the next batch (6 raw lines) gets laid out + the
    cursor advances + _contentHeight grows.
  - Scrolling DOWN past the currently-laid-out boundary clamps at the
    current bottom (LayoutProgress.clampedScroll). As batches complete
    the clamp relaxes and the user can continue scrolling.
  - After ~5-6 timer ticks (~400 ms wall clock), all 50 raw lines are
    laid out, `_layoutComplete` becomes true, and the "..." marker
    disappears. Article is fully scrollable end-to-end.

Tap any short fixture (e.g. "שיר", "שמש" — 2-3 raw lines each):
  - Layout completes in the first onUpdate (within the INITIAL_LINES=12
    budget). `_layoutComplete=true` on entry; "..." marker is never
    drawn. No timer scheduled. Behaves indistinguishably from a normal
    M2.x article load.

Press lower-right physical back button from the reader:
  - View pops (wikiwatchDelegate.onBack returns false; CIQ default).
  - wikiwatchView.onHide fires, stops _layoutTimer, sets it to null —
    no stray timer callbacks fire on the dead view.
  - Returns to the previous view (ResultsView or keyboard).

==============================================================================
LAZY-LAYOUT STATE MACHINE — RACE-CONDITION COVERAGE
==============================================================================
Monkey C is single-threaded; "races" are really event-sequencing cases.
Coverage:

  Case A: scroll ended BEFORE second-load started.
    User dragged to the laid-out bottom before the first timer tick.
    LayoutProgress.clampedScroll(targetY=9999, currentContentH, screenH)
    clamps at currentContentH - screenH. Tested by
    `layoutProgress_scrollEndedBeforeLoadStarted` (PASS).

  Case B: scroll ended WHILE second-load was in progress.
    User holds the scroll at the current bottom; a timer tick fires
    and grows contentH from C1 to C2. clampedScroll(scrollY=C1-screenH,
    C2, screenH) stays at the old position (still inside [0, C2-screenH]).
    Tested by `layoutProgress_scrollEndedWhileLoadInProgress` (PASS).

  Case C: scroll goes negative or content < screen.
    Defensive clamps. Tested by
    `layoutProgress_clampedScrollNegativeBecomesZero` and
    `layoutProgress_clampedScrollContentSmallerThanScreen` (PASS).

  Case D: view popped while timer scheduled.
    `onHide` stops the timer + nulls the field. Next fire would no-op
    (timer no longer scheduled). Verified in code; not unit-testable
    without view lifecycle simulation.
